* **Composition:** Eukaryotic chromosomes consist of DNA, histones（H1, H2A, H2B, H3, H4）, non-histones (scaffold proteins, transcription factors), and associated RNAs (like XIST).


* **Compaction Levels (折叠包装层级):**
1. **Nucleosomes (核小体):** DNA wrapped around core histones.

2. **Supercoiling (超螺旋):** A secondary theoretical compaction level.

3. **Radial Loop-Scaffold Model (放射环支架模型):** Formation of loops and rosettes.



* **Banding Patterns:** Giemsa staining reveals intrinsic alternating dark and light bands used to map gene locations via **Ideograms (染色体模式图)**.

* **3D Genome Mapping:** Techniques like **Hi-C** crosslink and ligate spatially close DNA fragments, followed by sequencing, to identify regions that are physically adjacent in 3D space despite being distant linearly on the sequence.

### **Chromatin Dynamics & Epigenetic Regulation (染色质动态与表观遗传调控)**

Highly compacted chromatin prevents transcription. The structure is dynamic and regulated by three main mechanisms:

1. **Chromatin Remodeling (染色质重塑):** Nucleosomes slide along the DNA to expose previously hidden promoter regions (e.g., SWI/SNF complex in yeast).


2. **Histone Modifications (组蛋白修饰):**
> * **Acetylation (乙酰化):** Alters electrical charge, weakening histone-DNA interactions; associated with active, transcriptionally rich genes.

> * **Methylation (甲基化):** Can either repress or activate gene expression by creating binding sites for specific activators or repressors.




3. **Histone Variants (组蛋白变体):** Replacing standard histones. For example, **CENP-A** (a variant of H3) replaces normal histones at the centromere to define kinetochore function.

### **Heterochromatin & Position Effect Variegation (异染色质与位置效应斑驳)**

* **Heterochromatin vs. Euchromatin:** Chromosomes have highly condensed, inactive regions (**Heterochromatin** - 异染色质) and less condensed, active regions (**Euchromatin** - 常染色质).


* **Position Effect Variegation (PEV - 位置效应斑驳):** Occurs when a normally active gene (like the *white* eye gene in flies) is moved near heterochromatin via an inversion event.


> * Heterochromatin can spread linearly, silencing the misplaced gene in some cells but not others, resulting in phenotypic mosaicism (e.g., mottled white and red eyes).



* **Barrier Elements (绝缘子/边界元件):** These elements recruit modifying factors to prevent the unregulated spreading of heterochromatin into euchromatic regions.

### **4: Mitosis, Meiosis & Gametogenesis (有丝分裂、减数分裂与配子发生)**

* **Cell Cycle:** Consists of G1, S phase (DNA replication), G2, and M phase (Mitosis).


* **Meiosis (减数分裂):** Contributes to genetic diversity through **Independent Assortment (自由组合)** of non-homologous chromosomes and **Recombination/Crossing Over (基因重组/交换)**.


* **Human Gametogenesis (人类配子发生):**

> * **Spermatogenesis (精子发生):** Begins at puberty, continues throughout life, and produces four mature sperm from one germ cell.


> * **Oogenesis (卵子发生):** Begins in the fetus. Oocytes are arrested in meiosis and mature one-by-one starting at puberty. Meiosis II is only completed upon fertilization, ultimately yielding just one large functional ovum per germ cell.

### **5: Chromosome Theory & Yeast Genetics (染色体学说与酵母遗传学)**

* **Morgan's Experiments:** Utilizing mutant *Drosophila* (white eyes), Morgan demonstrated **Criss-cross Inheritance (交叉遗传)**, proving that specific genes (like eye color) reside on the X chromosome.


* **Non-disjunction (染色体不分离):** Morgan's student (Calvin Bridges) found rare flies (1 in 2000) that defied expected ratios (e.g., white-eyed daughters from a red-eyed father). He proved this was due to homologous X chromosomes failing to separate during meiosis, providing ultimate proof for the Chromosome Theory of Inheritance.


* **Yeast as a Model Organism (from Guest Video):** Yeast is powerful for studying the cell cycle using **Temperature-sensitive Mutants (温度敏感突变体)**. These mutants function normally at 22°C but halt at a specific cell cycle stage at 36°C, allowing researchers to pinpoint genes responsible for cell division (like *CDC28*, a cyclin-dependent kinase).
